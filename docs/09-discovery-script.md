# 09 — Customer discovery script

This doc is **founder reference only** — it doesn't drive any code. It exists in the repo so the discovery findings, the design partner list, and the validation evidence stay attached to the project that resulted from them.

If you're Claude Code: skim this for context on user motivations, then ignore it. Don't generate code from this file.

## Goal

Confirm three things in 15–20 calls:

1. Monthly client reporting is in the top 3 monthly pains for boutique PR teams (not just "annoying").
2. They would pay $39–99/month for a tool that fully solves it.
3. At least 5 of them will pay something now to be design partners and shape v1.

If you finish 20 calls without 5 paid LOIs, the wedge is wrong. Stop and re-pick.

## Who to interview

- 8–10 boutique-agency owners or account leads (2–10 person agencies)
- 5–7 solo freelance PR pros with three or more retainer clients
- 2–3 in-house comms managers at companies that work with an outside PR agency (you're checking the report from the receiving end)

Recruit via LinkedIn search ("PR consultant," "founder + PR"), PRSA Slacks, Muck Rack and PR Daily comment sections, ProfNet, your network. Offer $50 Amazon card or a free year of the product.

## LinkedIn outreach

> Hey [Name] — I'm researching how small PR teams handle monthly client reporting. Not selling anything; I'm in the design phase of a tool and trying to learn from people who actually build these reports. 25 minutes, on Zoom, $50 gift card or a free year of whatever I end up shipping. Worth a chat?

Reply rate target: 10–15%. Lower = the message is too sales-y or your title is wrong.

## Mom Test ground rules

Default failure mode is the interviewee being polite. Three rules that matter:

1. Talk about their life, not your idea. Don't pitch in the first 30 minutes.
2. Ask about specifics in the past, not opinions about the future. "Walk me through your last report" beats "would you use a tool that…"
3. Talk less, listen more. If you're talking more than 30% of the call, you're leading the witness.

## 45-minute structure

| Time | Phase |
|---|---|
| 0–5 | Warm-up. Role, agency size, tenure. |
| 5–20 | The workflow. "Walk me through how you built your last monthly client report. Open the actual deck." Most important segment. |
| 20–30 | The wider workflow. Pitch tracking, monitoring, journalist research. |
| 30–38 | Show the mockup. "Can I show you a 60-second preview?" Watch their face. Then: "What's missing?" |
| 38–43 | Willingness to pay. "I'm looking for 5 design partners. $20/mo for 6 months, locked-in for life. Interested?" Real signal: do they get out their card. |
| 43–45 | Ask for referrals. |

## Question bank

**On their last report:**

- How long did your last monthly client report take, end to end?
- Which tools did you open? Walk me through each one.
- What's the part that takes longest? Why?
- What's the part you hate most? Why?
- If a junior person built it, how much time would you spend reviewing/fixing?
- What did the client actually do with the report when you sent it?

**On tools they've tried:**

- Which PR tools are you paying for right now?
- What's the most you've ever paid for PR software? Was it worth it?
- What did you try and stop using? Why?
- If your subscription doubled tomorrow, would you renew?

**On the deeper job:**

- What does a "good month" look like for you and your client?
- How does your client decide whether you're doing a good job?
- When was the last time a client churned? Why?
- What would 10 hours back per month let you do?

## Signal-quality scoring

Score each call 1–5 immediately after on three axes:

- **Pain intensity** — emotional language? a workaround they built themselves?
- **Solution-shape match** — did the mockup land? Did they unprompted name a feature you hadn't shown?
- **Willingness to pay** — yes to design partner offer? "When can I have it?"

A 4+ on all three = design partner. A 4+ on pain only = future user. Anything else = move on.

## Don'ts

- Don't pitch the product as a solved thing.
- Don't show the mockup before the workflow walk-through.
- Don't take "that's interesting" as signal.
- Don't skip the willingness-to-pay test.
