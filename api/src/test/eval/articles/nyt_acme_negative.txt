Acme Corp Lays Off 15% of Workforce After Missing Targets

By A. Reporter · November 18, 2025

Acme Corp, the workflow automation startup that raised a $30 million Series B last year, told employees on Tuesday that it would eliminate roughly 75 jobs, or about 15 percent of its workforce. The cuts are concentrated in sales and marketing, according to two people familiar with the matter.

The layoffs come after the company missed its revenue targets for the third consecutive quarter, the people said. CEO Jane Doe said in an internal memo that the company would refocus on its enterprise customer base and pause its mid-market expansion. "These were necessary decisions to ensure Acme can grow sustainably," she wrote.

A spokesperson for Acme confirmed the workforce reduction but disputed the characterization of missed targets, saying the company exceeded its enterprise pipeline goals. Investors expressed disappointment in the company's recent performance, two people said. Severance packages will include three months of pay and continued health benefits.
