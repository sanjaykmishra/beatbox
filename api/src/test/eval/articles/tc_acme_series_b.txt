Acme Corp raises $30M Series B led by Sequoia

By Sarah Perez · December 4, 2025

Acme Corp, the AI-driven workflow automation startup, announced today that it has closed a $30 million Series B funding round led by Sequoia Capital, with participation from existing investors Bessemer Venture Partners and Ribbit. The round brings Acme's total funding to roughly $50 million since the company was founded in 2023.

Acme's CEO Jane Doe said the new capital will be used to triple the engineering team and accelerate development of the company's enterprise tier, which has seen rapid uptake among financial services and healthcare customers. "We're going to use this to deepen our integrations and ship faster," Doe said in an interview.

Sequoia partner Roelof Botha said the firm was attracted to Acme's pace of execution and the strong renewal rates among its initial customer cohort.

The Series B values Acme at roughly $200 million post-money, according to two people familiar with the deal. The company declined to comment on valuation.

Acme plans to expand its San Francisco headquarters and open a New York office in early 2026 to support enterprise sales and customer success.
